## 2. Naming Patterns

1. **"sim" embedded/suffixed (most crowded):** aloSIM, Yesim, BNESIM, Simify, Sim Local, eSIM.me, eSIM4Travel, Esimatic — plus every aggregator. Functional but generic; hard to trademark; pre-frames you as a commodity.
2. **Roam/air/sky/jet compounds (second most crowded):** Roamless, BetterRoaming, KnowRoaming, Flexiroam, Roamify, Airalo, Airhub, GigSky, Jetpac, Voye. "Roam-" is near-saturated.
3. **Short invented friendly two-syllable words (the winning pattern):** Airalo, Saily, Ubigi, Holafly, Firsty, Truely, Ohayu, Nomad. The four biggest consumer successes all avoid "sim" entirely. Brandable, app-store-friendly, own their SERP.
4. **Borrowed-word plays:** Nomad (lifestyle), Ohayu (Japanese "good morning"), Holafly ("hola" + "fly").

**Crowded:** *sim*, *roam*, *air*, *global*, *go*. **Open:** warm invented coinages; journey-moment words (layover, departure, boarding); greeting words in other languages; comfort metaphors. The market rewards a *consumer brand* name, not a descriptive telecom name.

## 3. Design Audit (top 9 sites)

**Airalo** — Hero: destination-first; homepage centers a **search box that autocompletes countries** and routes to `/{country}-esim` pages. Nav splits Local / Regional / Global. Trust: app badges, App Store 4.6 / Play 4.4 — but web Trustpilot only ~4.0, a visible trust gap. Visuals: white background, navy text, coral/orange accent, rounded sans, flag-chip cards. Weakness: choice overload; near-identical packs; support reputation lags app polish.

**Holafly** — Hero: benefit-first ("unlimited data") rather than search-first; heavy Trustpilot badge usage. Visuals: signature bright pink/magenta on white, illustration-heavy. Simplest pricing in the market (pick days, not GB) — a major conversion advantage. Weakness: priciest; programmatic city pages are SEO-thin duplicates; VoIP-only calling buried in fine print.

**Saily** — Hero: clean destination search + "from $X" anchoring (`saily.com/esim-japan/`). Trust: Nord parentage, 4.7 Trustpilot, security freebies. Visuals: dark/near-black surfaces with vivid lime accent, geometric sans — the most "designed" brand in the set. Weakness: thinner editorial moat; coverage quality varies.

**Nomad** — "International data eSIMs in 200+ destinations." QR-less one-tap install is the UX benchmark. Visuals: airy white, soft purple-blue, destination card grid. Weakness: no unlimited; web subordinate to app.

**Jetpac** — Travel companion, not data pipe: perks (lounge on delay, fast-track, AI itinerary). Bold retro-travel aesthetic, lime-and-dark palette. Best differentiation; weakness: perks bury the core purchase, pricing clarity suffers.

**aloSIM** — Friendly Canadian brand; clean `/{country}-esim/` pages; pioneer of `/llm-info/` for AI assistants. "Solid, unremarkable."

**MobiMatter** — Marketplace: unbeatable prices, **cost-per-GB sort** (unique, loved by power users), cashback. Weakness: clutter, inconsistent provider quality, intimidating for first-timers.

**Yesim** — App-first; PAYG + VPN + virtual numbers. Purple gradients, app-screenshot-led homepage. Website secondary to app.

**Maya Mobile** — One flat unlimited price everywhere ($3.33/day), clean `/plans/{country}` URLs. Weakness: smaller coverage, thin content/SEO.

### Common weaknesses across the market (our openings)
- **The QR "two-screen problem"** — activation friction is the #1 complaint category industry-wide; most checkouts still end in a QR email.
- **Choice overload & opaque pricing** — leaders bury per-GB economics; only MobiMatter shows cost-per-GB; throttling terms hide in FAQs.
- **Thin programmatic pages** — duplicated country copy; no real local info (networks, speeds, 5G status, airport tips).
- **Web/app trust gap** — Airalo's 4.0 web Trustpilot vs 4.6 app rating.
- **Weak post-purchase UX** — usage dashboards and top-ups are app-only for most brands; web account areas are afterthoughts.

## 4. SEO & AI-Search Strategy Audit

**What leaders rank for:** head terms ("esim", "travel esim") plus a huge programmatic long tail of "esim {country}" / "best esim for {country}". High-intent geo long-tail is the lower-competition opportunity zone.

**Verified architectures:**
- **Airalo:** flat `/{country}-esim` (~200 money pages) + regional/global hubs + blog + help center.
- **Saily:** `/esim-{country}/` + aggressive **competitor-review blog** (`/blog/airalo-vs-holafly/` etc.) + Nord ecosystem cross-links — a domain-authority flywheel competitors can't copy.
- **Holafly:** `/esim-{destination}/` + `/reviews/{competitor}-review/` + `/{a}-vs-{b}/` + multilingual FAQ + programmatic city/state pages (quantity over quality).
- **Jetpac:** `/destinations/` + `/blog/best-esim-for-{country}/` — directly targeting affiliate-blog keyword space.
- **Reviewing your rivals on your own domain is table stakes in this category.**

**Affiliate layer:** Airalo ~10% (reports of 20–35% for some tiers); Holafly 10%; norms $8–30/sale or 15–35% rev-share. Nearly every "best eSIM for {country}" SERP result is an affiliate post; rankings partly reflect commission generosity.

**Schema.org:** leaders show rich results (price "from $4.00" in Airalo snippets) indicating Product/Offer markup; FAQPage and AggregateRating recommended sitewide.

**AI search (AEO/GEO) — the clearest white space:** only **aloSIM** publishes an `/llm-info/` page for ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity. llms.txt adoption in this vertical is near zero. ChatGPT search referrals grew ~740% in 12 months. No major brand offers a machine-readable plan/price feed for AI agents.

**Exploitable gaps:**
1. Country pages with *real* local substance (network names, measured speeds, 5G status, airport tips).
2. An honest, data-driven comparison hub (current vendor "reviews" are transparently biased; a fair one earns links).
3. Price-per-GB transparency, presented beautifully.
4. AEO: llms.txt + structured plan feeds + quotable stat pages.
5. Under-served intents: cruises, multi-country itineraries, "esim for {airport}", "esim with phone number", monthly/nomad subscriptions.

## 5. Recommendations

### 5a. Ten brand-name ideas
*(Avoid "sim/roam/air/go" compounds — saturated. Follow the Airalo/Saily/Holafly model: warm, two-syllable, invented or journey-moment words. Domain/trademark availability NOT verified — check every one.)*

1. **Layova** — from "layover"; travel-moment word, no telecom cliché.
2. **Departa** — boarding-gate energy; works in Romance languages.
3. **Embarka** — "embark"; optimistic, verb-driven.
4. **Sojo** — from "sojourn"; short, app-icon-friendly.
5. **Farlo** — "far" + friendly -lo ending (the Airalo pattern).
6. **Jauntly** — "jaunt" + the Saily-style -ly suffix.
7. **Tarmo** — from "tarmac"; punchy.
8. **Enroya** — from "en route"; premium-sounding.
9. **Wyfar** — "wayfarer" compressed; Gen-Z skew.
10. **Pakkit** — pun on data packets + packing; playful (riskiest legally).

**Chosen (June 2026): Layova** (#1 above) at **layova.travel** — vetted with no existing brand/app/eSIM collisions found. Configured in `lib/site.ts`.

### 5b. The hero formula to beat them
Combine Airalo's destination-search with Saily's price-anchoring and Holafly's simplicity — none of them do all three:
- **H1:** outcome + price anchor ("Landing in Japan? Data from $3.49 — installed in 60 seconds").
- **One giant destination search bar** with flag autocomplete, geolocation pre-fill, and inline from-price in the dropdown.
- **Trust strip directly under the search:** Trustpilot stars + count, app ratings, "instant delivery", refund/free-trial badge.
- **Price-per-GB on every plan card** — steal MobiMatter's one great idea and present it beautifully.
- No carousel, no stock-photo montage; a single product-real visual; sub-2.5s LCP budget.

### 5c. Programmatic SEO architecture to outrank them
- **Money pages:** `/esim/{country}/` (~200), each with unique data: networks + 5G status, measured speeds, plan table with per-GB pricing, country-specific FAQ, traveler tips. Quality bar = beat eSIMDB's data, not Holafly's thin pages.
- **Second-tier spokes:** `/esim/{region}/`, `/esim/{country}/unlimited/`, `/esim/{airport}/` for top ~50 airports, cruise pages.
- **Comparison layer:** `/compare/{a}-vs-{b}/` and `/alternatives/{brand}/` — genuinely accurate, dated, data-table-driven.
- **Editorial:** "best eSIM for {country}" guides, destination connectivity guides, an annual "State of Roaming Prices" report as a linkable asset.
- **Technical:** Product + Offer + FAQPage + AggregateRating schema sitewide; hreflang from day one; fast static rendering.
- **AI-search moat (biggest near-term arbitrage):** llms.txt, an `/llm-info` page, a public machine-readable JSON plan feed, and one-paragraph extractable answers on every country page.
- **Affiliate program at 20–30%+ with a self-serve portal** — under-commissioning guarantees invisibility in "best esim for X" SERPs.

### 5d. Trust & conversion elements
- Trustpilot widget with live count (volume matters: leaders show 13k–31k reviews) — seed reviews from day one.
- Free trial data (100–200MB) before payment — proven by Airhub/Voye/Firsty, rare among leaders.
- No-QR one-tap install + "works before you land" messaging; interactive "Is my phone eSIM compatible?" tool (big adjacent search volume).
- Transparent throttling/FUP terms on the plan card, not the FAQ.
- Money-back guarantee for failed activation; 24/7 human chat.
- **Great web dashboard** (usage bar, low-data alert, one-click top-up) — almost everyone hides this in the app; an open differentiator.
- One "beyond data" perk (lounge-on-delay or VPN bundle), kept secondary to a frictionless purchase.
