# Layova B2B Go-To-Market Playbook

*Travel eSIM — B2B/partnership revenue without capital. NZ-based. Researched June 2026.*

The thesis: agencies get only ~10% from Airalo/Holafly affiliates while they're used to 15–35% on ancillaries. eSIM gross margins (60–80% at retail) let a lean brand pay partners 20–25% and still profit. Sell through people who already own travelers.

---

## 1. Competitor B2B/partner programs (verified)

| Player | Offer to partners | Commercials | Friction |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Airalo Partners** | Affiliate links, reseller platform (co-brand, assign eSIMs from dashboard), API/SDK, Airalo for Business | Affiliate ~**10%** (via Impact); resellers buy at net pricing, min selling price applies | Low |
| **Holafly Partners** | Travel Partners affiliate for agencies, API, white-label, co-brand, marketing kits | **10% per eSIM**, free to join, 365-day cookie; general affiliate ~7% | Very low |
| **1GLOBAL** | Telco-as-a-Service white-label, enterprise plans | Shared pools 50GB–1TB; unlimited ~€57/mo; quote-only | Medium (enterprise motion) |
| **Jetpac (Circles)** | Consumer-led; B2B co-brands exist (DBS bank) but no public program found | — | High/opaque |
| **Maya Mobile** | Reseller/white-label; resell pre-made or build own plans | Claims no setup fees / no minimums / no contract; pricing under NDA | Low |
| **MobiMatter** | Reseller portal (wholesale, bulk, white-label) + affiliate | Affiliate **10%**; reseller wallet min **US$250** — lowest entry found | Very low |

**Insight:** the giants under-pay the channel. Our `/business` page offers **20%** — double the market — and it costs us nothing until a sale happens.

## 2. Aggregator shortlist (our supply, ranked for zero capital)

1. **eSIM Access** — near-zero deposit (Stripe accepted), white-label API, 100+ countries. *Start here, launch in days.*
2. **MobiMatter Reseller** — US$250 wallet; multi-supplier marketplace; cherry-pick best per-corridor SKUs with no contract. *Run in parallel.*
3. **eSIM-Go** — US$1,000 top-up commitment over first 3 months, then no minimum on Standard; best platform/docs. *Adopt once 2–3 group bookings cover it.*
4. **Maya Mobile** — claims no fees/minimums; sign the NDA early as second source + Pacific gap-filler.
5. Later: **Telna / 1GLOBAL / BICS** at real volume.

**Rule: never single-source.** Two wallets minimum so a coverage gap never kills a group order.

**Wholesale economics (verified ranges):** ~US$0.60–0.80/GB mid-volume in cheap markets; Japan low-data bundles from ~US$1.50. Retail Fiji eSIMs sell at US$17–25 for 3–10GB → 60–80% gross margins are normal.

**⚠ Pacific caveat (our corridor thesis's biggest risk AND moat):** majors cover Fiji, French Polynesia, Tonga, Vanuatu, PNG; **Samoa is patchy and the Cook Islands are essentially uncovered by international eSIMs today** (local Vodafone SIM only). Verify Pacific coverage country-by-country with each aggregator BEFORE promising it; price Pacific SKUs only after seeing actual wholesale rates.

## 3. The pain we undercut (NZ/AU roaming, verified June 2026)

- **Spark NZ:** NZ$11.50 per 200MB casual (≈ **NZ$57.50/GB**) in Pacific destinations
- **One NZ:** NZ$10/day · **2degrees:** NZ$8/day (1GB/day then throttled)
- **Telstra:** AU$10/day (AU$5/day NZ/Pacific) · **Optus:** ~AU$5/day
- 14-day Japan trip on NZ daily roaming = **NZ$112–140/person**; a 10GB Japan eSIM retails NZ$25–40 and costs ~US$8–15 wholesale. That's the wedge in every pitch.

## 4. First three segments to pitch (in order)

1. **School & sports trip organizers** (NZ/AU → Japan, Fiji, Pacific, UK/EU). One decision-maker, 15–50 eSIMs per order, one invoice, genuine safety pitch (parents can reach kids). Named NZ targets: **Tour Time, Haka Sports Tours, Student Horizons, Educating Adventures.** Nobody owns this niche.
2. **Independent travel agencies** (House of Travel owner-operators, helloworld members, YOU Travel, home-based agents). They monetize ancillaries at 15–35%; offer 20–25% co-branded and we beat Airalo/Holafly on the only number they care about. Flight Centre already bundles Hubby eSIM (proof of channel appetite); the independents are unserved — no HOT/helloworld eSIM deal found (recheck before pitching).
3. **SMEs with traveling staff** (exporters, trades, film, agritech, Pacific-facing NGOs/churches). Corporates pay US$80–200/traveler/week roaming; eSIM platforms run US$10–30. Incumbents force quote requests; we win with "one NZD invoice, GST receipt, eSIMs in inboxes today."

*Deprioritize for now:* cruise retail (confusing port-day-only pitch), big chains (procurement, being locked up), DMCs (slow cycles — revisit day 90).

## 5. The pitch (template)

> **Subject: Your clients are paying Spark $57/GB in Fiji — give them a better option and keep 20%**
>
> Hi [name] — Spark charges $11.50 per 200MB in Raro/Fiji; One NZ is $10/day. A Layova travel eSIM does the same trip for under $30 total, installed before they leave your office.
>
> The offer: a free co-branded page with your logo. Every sale through it pays you **20% commission**, tracked automatically, paid monthly. No stock, no setup cost, no support burden — we handle installs and help. (Airalo and Holafly pay agents ~10%; we pay double.)
>
> Group bookings (schools, sports, weddings): one invoice, every traveler gets a QR by email, **15% off retail for 10+ travelers**, and you still earn your 20%.
>
> 15 minutes this week to set up your page?

**Commercial menu** (lead with #1):
1. **Co-branded referral:** partner earns 20% (25% at 50+ sales/mo). Zero risk both sides.
2. **Wholesale + markup:** operator buys at retail −30%, one invoice, min 10 eSIMs, sets own price.
3. **White-label portal:** partner's brand end-to-end — only for partners proving 100+/mo.

**Anchor retail (NZD, sanity-checked):** Japan 10GB ~$35 · Europe 10GB ~$39 · Fiji 5GB ~$35. Funds a 20% commission at 40–55% gross on major corridors.

## 6. Revenue per partner (projections, assumptions flagged)

| Partner | Assumption | Monthly result |
|---|---|---|
| 1 modest agency | 15 sales × NZ$35, 20% out, ~35% COGS | ~NZ$235 net |
| 1 active agency | 50 sales × NZ$35 | ~NZ$790 net |
| 1 school-trip organizer | 4 trips/yr × 25 pax × NZ$30 net | ~NZ$1,500–1,800/yr |
| 1 SME (10 travelers) | NZ$30/traveler/mo | ~NZ$180–200 |

**Ten mixed partners ≈ NZ$2,500–4,000/mo gross profit** — and it compounds by adding partners, not capital. (Projections; no public per-partner benchmarks exist.)

## 7. 30/60/90-day plan

**Days 1–30 — Supply + proof**
- Open eSIM Access + MobiMatter wallets (≤US$300 total). Sign Maya NDA.
- Pull real wholesale rates: Japan, Fiji, Australia, Europe, USA, Samoa/Tonga/French Polynesia. Kill any SKU that can't margin.
- Test eSIMs on own phones; document install flow.
- Wire the real provider into `lib/provider.ts`; connect Stripe test keys.
- List 30 targets: 10 school/sports operators, 10 independent agencies, 10 SMEs from own network.

**Days 31–60 — First partners**
- Pitch all 30. Goal: **5 referral partners + 1 group booking.**
- Over-deliver on the first school group (Zoom install session, WhatsApp support line, free teacher eSIM) → case study.
- Publish two proof pages: "Fiji roaming vs eSIM: what Spark/One NZ really charge" and "Connectivity for school trips to Japan."

**Days 61–90 — Systematize**
- Move best-corridor volume to eSIM-Go Standard once bookings cover the US$1k.
- Automate fulfilment + monthly commission report (spreadsheet is fine; pay on time, always).
- Pitch 3 mid-size wholesale targets.
- **Day-90 gate:** ≥8 partners and ≥NZ$3k/mo GMV → invest in white-label tooling; otherwise double down on school/sports only.

## 8. Verify before relying on it

eSIM Access deposit floor + per-GB rates (quote-gated) · Maya "no minimums" (marketing claim) · Pacific coverage per aggregator (esp. Samoa, **Cook Islands — likely impossible via eSIM today**) · current NZ carrier rates · whether HOT/helloworld have since signed an eSIM deal.
